# Hero Video Candidate Concepts

Date: 2026-06-11

Brand target: Blush should feel like a luxury floral atelier, closer to perfume or jewelry cinematography than a normal flower shop.

## A. Macro Bloom Opening

- Emotional hook: The private moment before beauty becomes a gift.
- First 1-second impact frame: Extreme macro of a blush garden rose or peony petal edge, ivory highlights, tiny condensation, creamy shadow.
- 6-8 second storyboard: petal edge in focus, slow drift across layered folds, subtle natural breathing motion, focus lands on the flower center, camera eases back into a loop.
- Desktop 16:9 direction: bloom fills right 60%; left 35% stays calm and darker for text.
- Mobile 9:16 direction: bloom fills upper two-thirds; lower third remains soft and low contrast for copy.
- Camera movement: 100mm macro, very slow push-in, minimal parallax.
- Lighting: soft window side light, pale champagne highlight, no sparkle/glitter.
- Flower/product focus: blush garden rose, ranunculus, ivory orchid texture.
- Text-overlay safe area: left side desktop; lower third mobile.
- Loop strategy: camera motion reverses or eases back to the start position with no visible jump.
- AI artifact risk: waxy petals, impossible flower centers, fake dew, petal morphing.
- Why it may outperform: strongest instant beauty and thumb-stop value; most atmospheric.

## B. Florist Final Touch

- Emotional hook: The care you can feel before the bouquet arrives.
- First 1-second impact frame: elegant fingertips adjust one blush bloom; the gesture feels personal and deliberate.
- 6-8 second storyboard: hand enters, rotates one stem, smooths a leaf or wrap edge, presses ribbon/paper into place, pauses on complete bouquet.
- Desktop 16:9 direction: bouquet center-right, hand detail visible but not dominant, copy on left.
- Mobile 9:16 direction: tighter crop on hand, ribbon, and top blooms.
- Camera movement: 50mm or 85mm editorial close-up, locked frame with slight warmth.
- Lighting: natural studio light, soft shadows, warm cream highlights.
- Flower/product focus: finished Blush bouquet with one final human adjustment.
- Text-overlay safe area: clean paper/wall area on left or lower center.
- Loop strategy: hand exits, pause, then loop restarts with a similar hand entry.
- AI artifact risk: hand anatomy, extra fingers, distorted nails, impossible stem interaction.
- Why it may outperform: best for trust and craft; makes WhatsApp consultation feel premium.

## C. Luxury Arrangement Reveal

- Emotional hook: The moment the gift becomes unforgettable.
- First 1-second impact frame: premium arrangement emerges from shadow into ivory/blush light.
- 6-8 second storyboard: bouquet partially obscured, slow lateral reveal or turntable rotation, key blooms catch light, ribbon/vase texture appears, scene settles into hero composition.
- Desktop 16:9 direction: arrangement right or center-right, left copy zone.
- Mobile 9:16 direction: vertical bouquet crop, copy over calm wall/table or wrapping zone.
- Camera movement: 70mm product lens, slow lateral reveal or controlled turntable.
- Lighting: luxury still-life lighting, soft key, gentle rim, warm gold reflection.
- Flower/product focus: asymmetrical premium bouquet or vase arrangement with visible product clarity.
- Text-overlay safe area: wall/table/shadow gradient, never busy petals behind body text.
- Loop strategy: continuous slow rotation with matching start/end angle.
- AI artifact risk: can become generic luxury stock, overfilled bouquet, too glossy.
- Why it may outperform: clearest product comprehension and likely strongest conversion clarity.

## D. Soft Gold Packaging Ritual

- Emotional hook: The gift already feels special before it is opened.
- First 1-second impact frame: champagne ribbon slides across blush wrapping paper, catching warm light.
- 6-8 second storyboard: ribbon slides through fingers, paper folds around stems, ribbon crosses or tightens, final seal/bow lands, flowers peek from wrap.
- Desktop 16:9 direction: action lower/right, clean paper field left for copy.
- Mobile 9:16 direction: ribbon movement diagonally through frame, copy above/below action.
- Camera movement: 85mm close product shot, slightly overhead, slow controlled dolly.
- Lighting: warm late-afternoon gold, soft shadows, premium tabletop styling.
- Flower/product focus: paper grain, ribbon tension, partial bloom detail, luxury packaging.
- Text-overlay safe area: large clean paper surface.
- Loop strategy: simple ribbon pull can reset naturally; avoid complex knot formation.
- AI artifact risk: hand/ribbon logic errors, impossible bow movement, fake paper folds.
- Why it may outperform: most ownable for a gifting brand; raises perceived value beyond flowers.

## Recommendation

Primary production route: **C. Luxury Arrangement Reveal with B-level hand detail**.

Reason: it keeps the preferred Cinematic Bloom Ritual mood but adds stronger commercial clarity. Macro-only beauty is atmospheric, but Blush needs immediate product comprehension plus emotional premium craft.

Secondary tests:

- A for pure mood and thumb-stop beauty.
- D for highest gifting/packaging differentiation.
- B only if hand quality is excellent; otherwise reject.
