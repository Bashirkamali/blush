# Brand And UX Brain

## Brand Mood

Blush should feel luxurious, minimal, refined, emotional, formal, cinematic, boutique, high-end, architectural, floral, and seasonal. The mood should be calm and polished, not loud or discount-driven.

The landing page should feel like entering a curated floral vitrine: quiet confidence, soft light, precise details, and a sense that each arrangement has been composed with care.

## Visual Language

- Minimal layouts with strong visual hierarchy.
- Real floral imagery as the primary emotional surface.
- Cinematic video or photography with careful cropping.
- Soft overlays, subtle bloom, and restrained depth.
- Boutique editorial spacing rather than dense ecommerce clutter.
- Architectural framing and symmetry where useful.
- Small, precise luxury details instead of heavy decoration.

## Color System

Known brand color:

- Blush pink: `#f6d6e5`

Current Tailwind colors:

- `blush-pink`: `#f6d6e5`
- `blush-rose`: `#f8bbd9`

Recommended supporting direction:

- White and warm off-white for space and calm.
- Soft gold for scarce accent moments only.
- Neutral charcoal for high-legibility text.
- Gentle rose/pink as atmosphere, not as a full-page wash.

Avoid making the entire UI one-note pink. Blush pink should feel like a signature, not a flood.

## Typography Direction

Current direction:

- Playfair Display for elegant serif headlines.
- Inter/system sans stack for body copy.

Recommended direction:

- Use serif type for brand moments, section titles, and cinematic statements.
- Use clean sans-serif for body, CTA labels, product details, and operational text.
- Keep type sizes mobile-first and controlled.
- Avoid oversized hero typography unless the background composition supports it.
- Preserve letter spacing carefully; luxury should come from spacing and restraint, not illegibility.

## Motion And Animation Direction

Motion should feel cinematic and subtle:

- Slow video movement, soft fades, slight parallax, gentle hover scale.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Avoid playful bouncing, fast transitions, busy animated decorations, and constant movement.
- Use motion to reveal focus and atmosphere, not to distract from conversion.
- Any scroll-linked motion should be performance-safe on mobile.

## Photography And Video Direction

Use real Blush floral media whenever possible.

Preferred qualities:

- Clear product visibility.
- Soft daylight or cinematic warm light.
- Floral details, packaging, vitrine/display context, hands/scale if tasteful.
- Vertical or mobile-friendly compositions for campaign traffic.
- Seasonal arrangements and boutique interior cues.

Avoid:

- Generic stock flowers.
- Dark, overly blurred, or cropped images where the product cannot be inspected.
- Heavy compression artifacts.
- Repetitive images that make the vitrine feel accidental.
- Large unoptimized video files on mobile.

## Copywriting Tone

The voice should be:

- Formal but warm.
- Minimal but emotionally resonant.
- Premium, not salesy.
- Specific to Shiraz and Blush when useful.
- Clear about what the visitor can do next.

Good directions:

- "Daily Vitrine"
- "Bespoke floral pieces"
- "Seasonal arrangements"
- "Curated daily pieces"
- Persian guidance for local users when campaign language requires it.

Avoid:

- Generic luxury cliches.
- Over-explaining the brand.
- Discount language unless explicitly part of a campaign.
- Too many competing messages above the fold.

## Emotional Promise

Blush promises a curated floral experience that feels personal, elegant, and memorable. The visitor should feel they are choosing more than flowers: they are choosing taste, care, presentation, and a refined emotional gesture.

## Luxury Signals

- Real, high-quality floral media.
- Quiet whitespace and confident hierarchy.
- Consistent CTA styling.
- Fast load despite rich media.
- Polished Persian/English language handling.
- Clear contact paths.
- Elegant packaging and vitrine imagery.
- Accurate pricing or no pricing; avoid inconsistent placeholder pricing.
- No broken assets or dead anchors.

## Things To Avoid

- Generic ecommerce card grids.
- Loud gradients and over-saturated pink/purple UI.
- Decorative effects that do not support the flowers.
- Duplicate components or unplanned rewrites.
- Heavy videos that delay first impression.
- Mixed phone numbers or unconfirmed links.
- Placeholder canonical URLs, placeholder images, or filename-derived product copy.
- Treating Persian/RTL content as an afterthought.

## First-Time Visitor Feeling

A first-time visitor should immediately understand:

- This is Blush.
- Blush is premium and floral.
- The daily vitrine is curated and current.
- They can act now through WhatsApp, Instagram, or the website.
- The brand is local to Shiraz and trustworthy.

The ideal feeling is: "This is refined, current, and easy to contact."
